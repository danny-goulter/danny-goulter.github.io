Emotional Intelligence

September 13, 2021

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence is being able to effectively manage and control the effect your emotions have on your feelings and how they’re expressed in everyday life. Through improving our own Emotional Intelligence we can more accurately perceive and identify our own emotions and even better recognise those emotions in others. This in turn helps us interact with others in work, life, and love across the board. Through listening better and interpreting the words more clearly it helps you to have far more empathetically charged conversations.

How is it different to IQ?

IQ is our more rational, analytical, and logic-based area of the brain and it is expressed through how well we problem-solve and reason with an issue. IQ by its nature differs from EQ in that it can be measured in tests, stats, figures, and scores. They also differ in the ways a person collects information: someone with a lot of EQ will focus on the intent behind what is being said or written down and the ways in which that information might improve their wellbeing or help others; whereas someone who’s more IQ driven will collect and retain that information far more effectively and will be better suited for recalling or using that knowledge later. This can have drawbacks of course because sometimes forming an opinion on that information or how it might benefit others isn’t even considered.

In computing circles, A.I (Artificial Intelligence) exists already everywhere because information collection and retrieval is easy for a machine to replicate; but A.E.I (Artificial Emotional Intelligence) on the other hand is considered a new frontier in research, and a long, long way off from even being properly understood let alone implemented. The neural pathways required for emotional thought concepts are just so much more complex.

Why is EQ important?

It just has so many benefits, it can decrease your stress levels in life or work, help build better relationships where needed. Help a team work better, help individuals work more productively alone and converse better with others. Being more emotionally engaged helps you become a more rounded, and overall better human being. It is an innate human quality and ability and we should all embrace it as thinking and flourishing human beings.
